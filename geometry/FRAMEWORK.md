## Coherence, Recursion, and Representation

Throughout the analyses, problems arise when a **locally constrained frame** attempts to describe behavior generated by a **larger recursive structure**.

Geometric representations are used here to make that recursion explicit.

Key principles:

- **Coherence** refers to internally consistent behavior across scale
- **Recursion** refers to self-reference across levels, not simple repetition
- **Representation** refers to how these patterns are tracked, not what they “are”

## Circular Closure vs. Recursive Crossing

Two distinct structural behaviors recur across analyses:

### Circular Closure (π-based)

- Periodic return to the same state
- Common in oscillatory and rotational models
- Tracks local completeness
- Does **not** generate new scale or density

Circular closure is sufficient for describing many local behaviors but often fails to capture emergent structure.

### Recursive Crossing (φ-based)

- Return to a related state at a different scale
- Self-reference through mirroring
- Generates density or stabilization at intersections
- Tracks global structure across scale

Recursive crossing can be **represented** using φ (the golden ratio) because φ naturally encodes self-similarity under scaling.

This does not imply φ is fundamental—only that it is a convenient and consistent representational tool.

## Common Geometric Representations

The following geometric forms appear repeatedly as **models**:

### Tetrahedral Containment
Used to represent minimal stable containment and boundary conditions.

### Toroidal Circulation
Used to represent self-referential flow and continuity without endpoints.

### Rosette / Spiral Structures
Used to represent recursive crossing and return at different scales.

These forms are not treated as physical objects. They are **diagrams of relationship**.

## The XYAKANYAA XA Ratio (Operational Definition)

Several analyses make use of an operational scaling ratio referred to as the **XYAKANYAA XA Ratio**:


XA = (φ · c²) / h ≈ 2.19 × 10⁵⁰ Hz/kg

Where:
- φ is the golden ratio
- c is the speed of light
- h is Planck’s constant

This ratio is **not** asserted as a new fundamental constant.

It is used as a **stabilization reference** that relates:
- quantum scale (h)
- relativistic scale (c²)
- recursive geometric scaling (φ)

Operationally, XA functions as a bridge between frequency-based descriptions and mass-based descriptions in certain models.

If this ratio fails to generalize or reproduce results, it should be rejected.

## Levels of Description

Analyses use three **levels of description**, not dimensions:

- **Level 3 — Local Frame**  
  Behavior visible from within a constrained observational context

- **Level 4 — Structural Context**  
  Patterns that emerge when recursive or global structure is included

- **Level 5 — Stabilized Description**  
  Reformulations that hold local behavior and generating structure simultaneously

These levels describe **perspective expansion**, not spatial or metaphysical dimensions.
