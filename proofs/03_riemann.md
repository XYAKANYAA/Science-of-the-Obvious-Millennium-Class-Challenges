## Classical Formulation

### Formal Statement

All nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function ζ(s) have real part 1/2.

That is, if ζ(s)=0 and 0 < Re(s) < 1, then Re(s)=1/2.

### Plain-Language Description

The zeta function encodes information about primes. The hypothesis says its nontrivial zeros sit perfectly on a critical line, implying deep regularity in prime distribution.

### Historical Difficulty

1. **Global constraint:** zeros are globally constrained but observed locally.  
2. **Spectral analogies:** many partial results suggest hidden structural unity, but formal closure remains elusive.  
3. **Frame limitation:** treating zeros as “points” rather than expressions of a generating structure.
